Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profile 68

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Military members assigned to a Rank Profile 68 classification are eligible for various tax benefits and deductions. Understanding these benefits can help streamline the tax filing process and maximize potential tax savings. This article provides a comprehensive guide for military personnel in Rank Profile 68 to navigate their tax obligations.

In the United States, tax-filing guidelines for military personnel are governed by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS). When filing as a military member, it is essential to understand that tax filing status, deductions, and exemptions may differ from those applicable to civilians. Military personnel assigned to a Rank Profile 68 may be considered members of a "Combat Zone" or an "eligible theater of operations" under certain tax laws. This classification may affect the computation of tax liability and availability of certain tax benefits.

To file taxes correctly, military members with Rank Profile 68 must gather the following documents:

  • Form W-2, Wage and Tax Statement, from each employer;
  • Form 1099-G, Certain Government Payments, or other forms of government compensation;
  • Tax-related documents from personal investments, such as brokerage statements;
  • Proof of military orders and deployment assignments;
  • All relevant tax returns and supporting schedules from the previous year;
  • A copy of Form W-4, Employee's Withholding Certificate, and changes made to it during the year; and
  • A copy of any other relevant tax-related documents.

To file taxes accurately, military members in Rank Profile 68 should familiarize themselves with tax laws and regulations specific to their status. Military personnel deployed in designated combat zones, including Afghanistan, Iraq, and certain other countries, may qualify for special deductions such as the Combat Zone Bonus and Exclusion from Income. Additionally, Military personnel in Rank Profile 68 have access to certain tax credits, deductions, and exemptions. The following information highlights some key tax concepts and available benefits:

  • Combat Zone Bonus and Exclusion from Income: Military personnel deployed in designated combat zones receive a combat zone bonus and are exempt from paying taxes on their base pay, allowances, and hazard pay received during their deployment.
  • Military Housing Allowance (MHA): Military personnel living on base may deduct up to the MHA amount from their income. Military housing costs on base may be deducted against ordinary income, but only up to the maximum MHA allowed.
  • Federal Tax Credits: Military personnel may claim tax credits for education expenses, earned income tax credits (EITC), and retirement savings deductions, including the federal tax credit for qualified education expenses and the federal tax credit for first-time homebuyers.
  • Exclusion from income: Military personnel deployed to a combat zone or participating in a humanitarian mission may exclude their income received from certain allowances during the period of deployment from federal income tax.
  • Additional standard deduction: Military personnel and their spouses (if applicable) may be allowed an increased standard deduction when filing a joint tax return while claiming active-duty pay and certain types of housing and subsistence allowances.

When submitting tax returns, personnel in Rank Profile 68 must complete Form 1040, using Schedule 1 (Additional Income and Adjustments to Income), Schedule 3 (Non-refundable and Refundable Credits), and Schedule A (Itemized Deductions) where necessary. Military members may need to attach additional supporting documents, such as Form 8938 (Statement of Specified Foreign Financial Assets) or Form 8949 (Sales and other Dispositions of Capital Assets). Before filing, military personnel in Rank Profile 68 should consult the IRS website and consult with tax professionals or utilize tax preparation software to ensure accurate reporting and maximum available tax savings.
