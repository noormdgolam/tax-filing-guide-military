Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profile 85: A Comprehensive Overview

As a member of the military with a rank profile 85, understanding the tax filing process can be complex due to the unique circumstances of military life. In this guide, we will walk you through the essential steps and requirements for filing your taxes as a member of the military with a rank profile 85.

Basic Requirements and Eligibility

To be eligible for tax filing as a member of the military with a rank profile 85, you must meet the following criteria:

  • Be a member of the US military with a rank profile 85
  • Have served on active duty, reserve duty, or in a drill status during the tax filing year
  • Have received a military income, including basic pay, allowances, and bonuses
  • Have completed Form W-2 and Form W-4, or have been exempt from withholding
Tax Filing Deadlines and Extensions

The tax filing deadline for military personnel is typically April 15th of each year. However, if you are serving outside the United States, you may be eligible for an automatic two-month extension to file your taxes. To qualify, you must meet the following criteria:

  • Be a member of the US military and have served outside the United States during the tax filing year
  • Have been physically outside the United States for at least 330 days during the tax filing year

Additionally, if you are granted an extension, you will need to submit Form 4868, Application for Automatic Extension of Time to File U.S. Individual Income Tax Return.

Income and Adjustments to Income

As a member of the military with a rank profile 85, your income may include:

  • Basic pay
  • Bonuses and incentives
  • Allowances for housing and food
  • Combat pay

You may also be eligible for adjustments to income, including:

  • Student loan interest deduction
  • Alimony payments
  • Business expenses related to military service

Be sure to consult the IRS Military Order of Battle to determine which expenses are eligible for deductions and credits.

Tax Credits and Deductions

As a member of the military with a rank profile 85, you may be eligible for the following tax credits and deductions:

  • Military EIC (Earned Income Credit)
  • Military housing deduction
  • Combat pay deduction
  • Longevity pay deduction

Additionally, you may be eligible for the following deductions:

  • Mortgage interest deduction
  • Charitable contributions deduction
  • Home office deduction
Form 4852 and Form 8829

If you have missed pay or experienced a change in pay, you may need to complete Form 4852, Substitute for Form W-2, Wage and Tax Statement. Additionally, if you have qualified for a home office deduction, you will need to complete Form 8829, Expenses for Business Use of Your Home.

Getting Help and Resources

As a member of the military with a rank profile 85, you have access to a range of resources and support to help with tax filing, including:

  • The IRS's Military Order of Battle
  • The IRS's website, www.irs.gov
  • The IRS's Taxpayer Assistance Centers
  • The IRS's military hotline, 1-800-829-1040
