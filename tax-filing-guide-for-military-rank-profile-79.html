Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profile 79

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

The United States Internal Revenue Service (IRS) offers various benefits and deductions for military personnel, including those classified under a 'Military Rank Profile' of 79. This guide will outline the necessary steps and essential information for tax filing as a member of the military, specifically those holding a rank associated with this profile.

Tax Filing Requirements

As a member of the military holding a rank corresponding to 'Military Rank Profile 79,' you are required to file a tax return if your total income exceeds the standard deduction for your filing status. The most common filing statuses for military personnel include single, married filing jointly, married filing separately, head of household, and qualifying widow(er).

  • Standard Deduction: For tax year 2023, the standard deduction for single filing status is $13,850.
  • Filing Status: Your filing status determines your tax bracket and eligibility for deductions and credits. Refer to the IRS website or consult a tax professional for detailed information on which filing status applies to you.

Military Tax Benefits and Deductions

The IRS offers several tax benefits and deductions specifically for military personnel, which may apply to members holding a 'Military Rank Profile' of 79:

  • Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH): BAH is a tax-free allowance provided by the government to offset the cost of living expenses in your duty station. However, if you choose to rent a home instead of receiving BAH, you may deduct your actual housing expenses.
  • Military Basic Allowance for Subsistence (BAS): BAS is a tax-free allowance provided for food. If you choose to claim the standard deduction instead of itemizing your expenses, BAS is not considered part of your taxable income.
  • Uniform Allowance: Uniform expenses, including items purchased with your uniform allowance, are tax-deductible.
  • Meal and Lodging Allowance: Meal and lodging expenses incurred while attending a temporary duty (TDY) assignment are tax-deductible up to a maximum amount per meal and lodging.
  • Combat Pay: Combat pay is tax-free and does not need to be reported on your tax return.

Forms and Schedules Required

To complete your tax return, you will need to report your income, deductions, and credits using various IRS forms and schedules. The following forms are commonly used by members of the military holding a rank corresponding to 'Military Rank Profile 79':

  • Form 1040: This is the standard form for personal income tax returns.
  • Schedule C: Form 1040: This schedule is used to report business income and expenses when you are self-employed or have income from a side business.
  • Form 3903: This form is used to calculate the earned income tax credit (EITC) if you have qualifying children.
  • Form W-2: This form is used by your employer to report your compensation and taxes withheld.
  • Form 1098-E: This form is used to report student loan interest payments you made.

Consultation and Support

If you are a member of the military holding a rank associated with 'Military Rank Profile 79,' it is recommended that you seek guidance from a qualified tax professional or a military tax services organization. These organizations specialize in providing expert advice and assistance with tax preparation tailored to the military community.

The IRS provides tax assistance for military personnel through various channels, including:

  • IRS Publication 596: This publication provides guidance on the earned income tax credit (EITC) and other tax benefits for low- to moderate-income working individuals and families.
  • IRS Publication 503: This publication provides guidance on the standard mileage rate for car usage.
  • My Account: You can log in to 'My Account' on the IRS website to access personalized account information, view payment history, and receive tax tips and reminders.

Remember to keep all relevant documents, including receipts and W-2 forms, organized and easily accessible to ensure accurate completion of your tax return.
