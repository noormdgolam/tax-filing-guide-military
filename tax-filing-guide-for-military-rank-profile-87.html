Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profile 87

This section provides a detailed tax filing guide specifically for military personnel holding rank profile 87. As a service member, individuals in this profile are subject to unique tax rules and regulations.

Who is affected by Rank Profile 87?

Rank profile 87 typically applies to service members with a combination of experience, pay grade, and service length. For the purposes of this guide, we will use the term 'Affected Service Members' to refer to personnel in this profile.

Determining Military Income

Military income is taxed differently than civilian income. Affected service members should report their military income on Form 1040 or its variants. Key components of military income include:

  • Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH): BAH is taxed as ordinary income. It represents an estimate of the cost of housing in your location, and is typically paid by the military.
  • Basic Allowance for Subsistence (BAS): BAS is not subject to federal income tax but may be taxed at the state and local level. It represents an allowance for food and other expenses.
  • Special Pay: Certain types of special pay, such as hazardous duty pay, combat pay, and submarine pay, may be subject to federal income tax.
  • Allowances and Deductions: Service members may be entitled to various allowances, such as moving expenses or combat zone tax relief, which can affect their tax liability.

State and Local Taxation

Many states offer reduced or waived state income tax on military income. However, state and local tax laws are subject to change, so it is essential to review state-specific rules and regulations. Some key concepts to consider:

  • Exclusion of Military Income: Some states exclude military income from state tax liability, while others only tax a portion of it.
  • Alternative Residency Rules: States with alternative residency rules may consider a service member's home state to be their state of residency while stationed elsewhere.

Additional Tax Considerations

Affected service members should be aware of the following tax-related implications:

  • Combat Zone Rules: Service members serving in a combat zone may qualify for special tax relief and exclusion from income tax.
  • Bond Interest Exclusion: Certain interest earned on Series EE savings bonds may be tax-free for qualified education expenses or the purchase of a first home.
  • Homebuying Assistance: Service members may be eligible for tax credits and other incentives for buying a first home.

Tax Filing Resources

Military service members can find detailed tax information and resources through:

  • The Military Tax Guide: An official publication outlining tax rules and regulations for military personnel.
  • Military Personnel Tax Centers: Online resources providing tax guidance and support for affected service members.
  • Joint Military Tax Council: A collaborative effort offering tax relief and support for military service members.
