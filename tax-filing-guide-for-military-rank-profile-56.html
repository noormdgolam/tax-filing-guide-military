Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profile 56

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Are you a military personnel holding the rank of E-5, specifically Profile 56? If so, you must be aware of the tax implications associated with this rank profile. The military tax system can be complex, which is why it's essential to understand the tax filing requirements and deadlines specific to your profile.

Overview of Military Tax Filing Requirements

The military tax system is designed to accommodate the unique needs of service members, offering tax benefits and exemptions to reduce their tax liability. However, the tax filing requirements can be challenging to navigate, especially for those new to the military tax system.

Military personnel are subject to a combination of federal, state, and local taxes. The type and amount of taxes you pay will depend on your tax filing status, income, and other factors. As an E-5 personnel with Profile 56, you will be required to file a tax return using the standard form (IRS Form 1040) or the simplified option (IRS Form 1040EZ/SR).

Key Features of Profile 56

Profile 56 is a specific military pay grade profile that affects the way your income is calculated for tax purposes. As an E-5 personnel with this profile, you will receive basic pay and allowances that are subject to federal income tax. However, your combat pay, housing allowance, and subsistence allowance are excluded from taxable income, making it a significant tax advantage.

The key features of Profile 56 include:

  • Basic pay is taxable
  • Housing allowance and subsistence allowance are nontaxable
  • Tax-exempt items, such as combat pay, are allowed
  • Military housing allowance is exempt from state taxation in most states

Tax Filing Deadlines and Options

As a military personnel, you have several tax filing options and deadlines to consider. The filing deadline for federal individual tax returns is typically mid-April, but the deadline is automatically extended for military personnel serving in a combat zone. For state and local taxes, the filing deadline varies by jurisdiction.

You can choose to file your tax return using one of the following options:

  • IRS Free File: Free tax preparation software available to taxpayers with an AGI below $66,000.
  • Free Tax Clinics: Free or low-cost tax preparation services offered at many military bases and community centers.
  • Paid tax preparation services: Commercial tax preparation companies that offer preparation and filing services for a fee.

Additional Tax Tips and Resources

To ensure accurate tax filing and maximize your tax benefits, take note of the following tax tips and resources:

  • Keep accurate records of your income, expenses, and tax documents, including W-2s, 1098s, and military orders.
  • Consult the IRS Publication 3, Armed Forces' Tax Guide, for detailed information on tax implications and benefits for military personnel.
  • Contact the IRS or your local military tax office for assistance with tax filing, extensions, or other tax-related issues.

By understanding the tax implications of Profile 56 and following these guidelines, you can navigate the military tax system with confidence, maximize your tax benefits, and reduce your tax liability.
