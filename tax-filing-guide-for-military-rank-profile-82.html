Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profile 82: Special Considerations

Military personnel with the rank of E-3 or higher, and having served for more than 180 days, fall under the Military Rank Profile 82. This guide provides essential information on tax filing for those in this profile, considering their unique circumstances.

Gross Income Reporting

Members of the military with a rank of E-3 or higher, serving for more than 180 days, are entitled to a tax-free portion of their basic allowance for housing (BAH). This tax-free amount is subtracted from their gross income, which affects their taxable income. To report this adjustment, members of the military must complete Form 2555, Foreign Earned Income, and attach schedules 3 and 6. They will report their taxable income on line 21 of the Form 1040.

Foreign Earned Income Exclusion

As military personnel stationed abroad, they may be eligible for the foreign earned income exclusion. This allows them to exclude a certain amount of their foreign-earned income from taxation. To qualify, service members must meet one of the two residency tests: - Bona Fide Residence Test: They must be a resident of a foreign country or countries for an uninterrupted period that includes an entire tax year. - Tie-Breaker Test: If they meet the tests for both the Bona Fide Residence Test and the Physical Presence Test, they are considered a qualified individual. They must also complete Form 2555 and attach schedules 3 and 6 to report their foreign earned income and exclusion amounts.

Combat Zone and Hazardous Duties Pay

Military personnel serving in combat zones or hazardous duties receive special tax breaks. Combat zone pay is exempt from taxation, and hazardous duty pay is not subject to taxation. Members must report this pay on their tax return and claim the related tax exemptions. They must complete Form 215G, and attach it to their tax return to claim this exemption.

Additional Tax Credits and Deductions

The military offers several tax credits and deductions to service members: - The Overseas Combat-related Tax Exclusion - The Combat Zone Tax Exclusion - The Hazardous Duty Tax Exemption - The Moving Expense Deduction To claim these credits and deductions, service members must submit the appropriate forms and schedules with their tax return. They should keep all required documentation, such as receipts and military orders, to support their claims.

Key Documents and Deadlines

Service members should obtain the following documents to ensure accurate tax filing: - Form 2555, Foreign Earned Income - Schedule 3, Dependents - Schedule 6, Foreign Earned Income Exclusion - Form 215G, Combat Zone and Hazardous Duties Pay - Moving expense records and receipts The tax filing deadline for members of the military stationed in combat zones is the 180th day after they leave the combat zone. Otherwise, tax filing deadlines remain the same as for other taxpayers, with extensions available if necessary.

Conclusion

Members of the military with rank profile 82 require special tax considerations. Understanding these requirements and obtaining the necessary documentation ensures accurate tax filing and minimizes stress during the tax season. Consult with a qualified tax professional or the IRS to confirm the most up-to-date tax information and requirements specific to their situation.
