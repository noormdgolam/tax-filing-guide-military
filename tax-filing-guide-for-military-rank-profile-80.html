Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profile 80

In the United States Armed Forces, Military Rank Profile 80 refers to commissioned officers holding the rank of Lieutenant Colonel (O-5) in the Navy, Marine Corps, and Coast Guard, or Colonel (O-6) in the Army, and O-6 equivalent in the Air Force and Space Force. As a Military Rank Profile 80, it is essential to understand and comply with the tax laws governing your compensation and benefits.

Tax Filing Obligations

All Military Rank Profile 80 personnel are required to file annual income tax returns with the Internal Revenue Service (IRS). The tax year typically runs from January to December, and the federal income tax return is due on April 15th of the following year. Failing to file or paying taxes on time may result in penalties and interest charges.

Income Types

As a Military Rank Profile 80, you receive various types of income, including:

  • Basing allowances: The allowances received for living expenses while stationed at various bases.
  • Combat zone pay: Special pay received for service in combat zones.
  • Differential pay: An allowance received to offset the costs of pay for service in the military while deployed overseas.
  • Extraordinary hazard duty pay: Pay received for hazardous duties.
  • Foreign duty pay: An allowance received for service outside the United States.
  • Leave pay: Pay received during time off (leave) in lieu of regular pay.
  • Overseas housing allowance
  • Reimbursement for expenses while deployed, such as meals, transportation, and lodging.
  • Subsistence pay: Allowance for meals while performing duties.
  • Tricare benefits (if applicable)

Special Tax Deductions and Credits

As a Military Rank Profile 80, you may be eligible for various tax deductions and credits, including:

  • Combat pay exclusion: The combat zone pay and hazardous duty pay is excluded from taxable income.
  • Mortgage interest deduction: Interest on a primary residence mortgage and/or second home
  • Roth IRA contributions: Contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA, subject to income limits
  • State and local taxes deduction: State and local taxes paid (subject to the $10,000 SALT limit)
  • Tax credits for education and earned income
  • Travel expense deductions: Business-related expenses, including meals, transportation, and lodging.

Form 1040 and Schedule A

The tax return forms used by Military Rank Profile 80 personnel typically include the following:

  • Form 1040: The main income tax return form.
  • Schedule A: Itemized deductions, including mortgage interest, state and local taxes, and medical expenses.

Prior to filing, it is essential to gather the necessary forms and documents, including:

  • W-2 and W-2 forms for income received from military pay and allowances.
  • 1098 form for mortgage interest (if applicable).
  • 1099 forms for interest and dividends (if applicable).
  • Form 8938 (Statement of Specified Foreign Financial Assets)
  • Form 8949 (Sales and Other Dispositions of Capital Assets)

Tax Filing Resources

For accurate and up-to-date information on tax laws, visit the following websites:

  • Internal Revenue Service (IRS): irs.gov
  • Department of Defense (DoD) Tax Center: defensetravel.dod.mil
  • IRS Publication 3: Armed Forces' Tax Guide: irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/p3.pdf

For personalized tax advice, consult a tax professional or the Military OneSource program at militaryonesource.mil.
