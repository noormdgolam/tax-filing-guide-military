Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profile 76

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

The IRS provides various tax benefits and incentives to members of the United States Armed Forces, including those with specific military rank profiles, such as Military Rank Profile 76. This article provides an overview of the tax filing procedures and benefits available to individuals holding this rank profile.

Eligibility for Military Benefits

To be eligible for the tax benefits outlined below, service members must meet certain qualifications, including being part of Military Rank Profile 76, serving on active duty for 90 days or more, or being honorably discharged.

  • Served on active duty for 90 days or more during the tax year.
  • Were honorably discharged from the military.
  • Did not receive a dishonorable discharge.

Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH)

The Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH) provides financial assistance to military service members for housing expenses. This allowance is taxable as income and may affect tax returns. Individuals with Military Rank Profile 76 who receive BAH should claim a standard deduction and consider itemizing expenses for tax purposes.

BAH rates vary depending on location and rank. Service members may also deduct home utilities such as gas, electricity, water, and trash collection, even if they are included in rent or are paid by the landlord.

Tax Exemptions and Credits

Members of the United States Armed Forces may qualify for certain tax exemptions and credits, including the Military Exemption from State Taxes, Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC), and the Child Tax Credit.

  • Military Exemption from State Taxes: Many states offer exemptions from state income tax to military personnel stationed in those states.
  • Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC): Eligible service members may claim the EITC to reduce their tax liability.
  • Child Tax Credit: Eligible service members may claim the Child Tax Credit to reduce their tax liability.

Combat Zone Exclusion

The Combat Zone Exclusion provides a tax exemption on income earned while in a combat zone. Members of Military Rank Profile 76 who serve in a combat zone for the full tax year may exclude income earned during that time from their gross income tax calculation.

  • Service members serving in a combat zone may exclude up to $7,500 of income from their tax returns.
  • The exclusion applies to military pay received while in a combat zone.

Special Tax Considerations

Members of Military Rank Profile 76 may also be subject to special tax considerations related to their military income, including:

  • Combat pay exclusion.
  • Special duty pay.

Service members may need to complete Form 2106 or Form 2106-EZ to claim deductions related to these income types on their tax returns.

It is essential for military service members with Military Rank Profile 76 to review their specific tax situation with a qualified tax professional to determine the best course of action for minimizing their tax liability and claiming eligible tax credits and deductions.
