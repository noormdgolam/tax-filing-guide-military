Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profile 27

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

As a member of the military with Rank Profile 27, you may be eligible for various tax credits and deductions to reduce your tax liability. Before we dive into the tax filing guide, it's essential to understand your unique tax situation.

Rank Profile 27 includes officers in the ranks of Lieutenant Colonel to Colonel and above, as well as some specialized officer ranks like the Judge Advocate General (JAG) and Chaplain. Your tax situation may be affected by your income, deductions, and credits related to your military service.

Required Documents

  • Form W-2 from your base pay
  • Form 1099-DIV for investment income
  • Form 1099-INT for interest income
  • Form 1098 for mortgage interest and property taxes
  • Copies of receipts for medical expenses, charitable donations, and other itemized deductions

Filing Status

Your filing status will determine the tax rates you'll pay and the deductions you're eligible for. As a member of the military, you may choose to file as:

  • Single: If you're unmarried or separated from your spouse
  • Married Filing Jointly: If you're married and you and your spouse are filing together
  • Married Filing Separately: If you and your spouse are married but choose to file separately

Tax Deductions and Credits

You may be eligible for various tax deductions and credits as a member of the military. Some of the most common deductions and credits include:

  • Military basic housing allowance (BHA) deduction
  • Temporary Duty (TDY) expenses deduction
  • Deployment income exclusion
  • Combat zone pay exclusion
  • Education expenses credit for service members

Form 1040 and Schedule A

You'll report your income and calculate your tax liability on Form 1040. You'll also use Schedule A to report itemized deductions, such as medical expenses and charitable donations. Here are some key fields to complete:

  • Line 1-15: Report your income and withholdings
  • Line 16-21: Calculate your standard deduction or itemized deductions
  • Line 22-35: Complete Schedule A for itemized deductions

Form 8938 and Form 5329

If you have foreign financial assets or retirement accounts, you may need to file Form 8938 and Form 5329. Form 8938 reports foreign financial assets, such as bank accounts, investments, and real estate. Form 5329 is used to report penalties on retirement accounts, such as a traditional IRA or 401(k).

Keep in mind that you may need additional forms and schedules, depending on your specific tax situation.
