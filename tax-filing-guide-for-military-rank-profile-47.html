Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profile 47

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Military personnel and their families often face unique challenges when it comes to navigating the complexities of tax filing. As a member of Military Rank Profile 47, it's essential to understand the specific tax benefits and obligations associated with your role.

**Qualifying for Military Tax Benefits**

  • The Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA) expanded the eligibility for the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) to include members of the military. To qualify, you must have earned income, such as wages, tips, or self-employment income.
  • You may be eligible for the Combat Zone Tax Relief, which provides a tax credit for income earned while serving in a combat zone. This credit is calculated on Form 8918, Notice of Combat Zone Service.
  • As a member of Military Rank Profile 47, you may also be eligible for the Military Spouses Residency Relief Act (MSRRA), which allows military spouses to maintain their home state for tax purposes while living in a different state.

**Understanding Your Military Pay**

  • As a member of the military, your pay is considered taxable income, and you will receive a Form W-2 from your command at the end of the year.
  • You may also receive hazardous duty pay, which is taxable, as well as hostile fire pay, which is not subject to federal income tax but may be subject to state tax.
  • Subsistence allowances, such as Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH), are taxable, while allowances for meals, travel, and education expenses may be non-taxable.

**Filing Status and Dependents**

  • As a member of Military Rank Profile 47, you may claim single filing status if you are unmarried or unmarried filing separately.
  • If you are married and filing jointly, you and your spouse may qualify for the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) or the American Opportunity Tax Credit.
  • You may claim dependents, including your spouse and eligible children, on Form 1040.

**Special Tax Credits**

  • The Military Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH) exclusion allows military personnel to exclude up to $25,900 (for the 2022 tax year) of their BAH from taxable income.
  • The Military Child Tax Credit provides a tax credit of up to $1,000 per child under age 17, in addition to the regular Child Tax Credit.

**How to File**

  • Complete Form 1040, the standard form for personal income tax returns, and attach any necessary schedules, such as Form 8938, Statement of Specified Foreign Financial Assets.
  • Use Form 8822-B to notify the IRS of any address changes, including changes to your military duty station.
  • Take advantage of tax preparation resources, such as the IRS Free File program, which offers free tax preparation and e-filing for qualified military personnel.

**Additional Resources**

  • The IRS offers a wealth of information on military tax benefits and obligations, including Publication 531, "Reporting and Payment of Military Pay and Allowances."
  • The Defense Finance and Accounting Service (DFAS) provides guidance on military pay and benefits, including the Federal Tax Withholding Tables.
  • Contact your local IRS office or military tax consultant for personalized advice and support.
