Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profile 13

The tax filing process for military members can be complex, but this guide is designed to assist those serving under the rank of E-7 (Master Sergeant, Master Corporal, or equivalent) with their tax obligations.

Military personnel serving under this rank profile may have unique circumstances that impact their tax filing, such as multiple deployments, hazardous duty pay, and Combat Zone Tax Exclusion (Combat Zone). It is essential to consider these factors when completing their tax return.

Before diving into the tax filing process, it is crucial to gather necessary documentation. These may include:

  • W-2 forms from all employers, including the military
  • 1099 forms for any freelance work or other income
  • Military orders and deployment schedules
  • Hazardous duty pay records
  • Proof of any Combat Zone tax exclusion (e.g. Form 2555)

When filling out the tax return, consider the following:

Military Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH)

BAH is a tax-free allowance for rent or home ownership. When calculating your taxable income, subtract your BAH from your adjusted gross income (AGI). To claim the BAH exemption, complete Form 2555, and attach it to your tax return.

Combat Zone Tax Exclusion (Combat Zone)

If you are serving in a Combat Zone, some or all of your military pay is tax-free. To claim this exclusion, complete Form 2555 and indicate the number of months you served in the Combat Zone.

Hazardous Duty Pay

Hazardous duty pay is taxable income. However, you may be eligible for a special housing allowance that is tax-free. To determine the amount of tax-free hazardous duty pay, refer to IRS Publication 3, Armed Forces' Tax Guide.

Other Military Income

Other military income, such as allowances for education, subsistence, or quarters, is generally tax-free. However, if these allowances exceed your actual expenses, you must report the excess as taxable income.

When completing your tax return, use the "Combat Zone" checkbox to indicate your service. Additionally, select the "Tax Free Combat Pay" checkbox if applicable. When claiming the Combat Zone tax exclusion, attach Form 2555 to your tax return.

It is recommended that military personnel serving under rank profile 13 seek the assistance of a tax professional or the IRS Free File program for tax preparation and filing. These resources can provide personalized guidance and help navigate potential deductions and exemptions.

When consulting a tax professional or using the IRS Free File program, be prepared to provide the necessary documentation, as outlined earlier. Additionally, take advantage of the IRS's free tax help resources, including Tax Help for Members of the Armed Forces (TAMAF), to ensure compliance with tax regulations.

Remember, the tax filing process for military members can be complex. By understanding the unique factors at play and seeking professional guidance, you can ensure accurate and compliant tax filing.

Important Tax Deadlines for Military Members:

For tax return preparation, file electronically by April 15th. For a paper return, mail your return by April 15th, but also allow an additional 6-8 weeks for processing.

Military Tax Relief Programs:

Several tax relief programs are available to military members, including the Combat Zone tax exclusion, tax-free hazardous duty pay, and the Special Allowance for Education, Subsistence, or Quarters. Understanding these programs can help navigate potential deductions and exemptions.
