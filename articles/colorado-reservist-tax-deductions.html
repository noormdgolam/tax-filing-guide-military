Colorado National Guard and Reservists Tax Deductions

The Colorado National Guard and Reservists Tax Deductions aim to provide financial relief to military personnel and their families, acknowledging the unique challenges they face while serving their country. This article provides an overview of the tax deductions available to those serving in the Colorado National Guard and Reserves.

What is a Reserve Components Relief Act (RCRA) Deduction?

The RCRA deduction allows qualifying reservists to deduct up to $3,000 of their income that was reduced due to their military service. This deduction is only available if the reservist's income was reduced by the amount of their military pay, and the deduction is not available for the first five years of service.

Who is Eligible for the RCRA Deduction?

  • Members of the Colorado National Guard or Reserves who received a call or order to active duty for at least 179 days in a calendar year.
  • Members of the Colorado National Guard or Reserves who received a mobilization order for at least 179 days in a calendar year.
  • Commissioned officers of the Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps, or Coast Guard who received a call or order to active duty for at least 179 days in a calendar year.
  • Commissioned officers of the Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps, or Coast Guard who received a mobilization order for at least 179 days in a calendar year.

How to Claim the RCRA Deduction

To claim the RCRA deduction, reservists must file Form 1098-E with their federal income tax return. The form must be completed and attached to the reservist's tax return, along with supporting documentation of their reduced income and military service.

Other Tax Credits and Deductions Available to Reservists

  • The Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC): A tax credit for eligible low- to moderate-income working individuals and families, which includes reservists.
  • The American Opportunity Tax Credit: A tax credit for education expenses incurred by the reservist or their family.
  • The Child and Dependent Care Credit: A tax credit for childcare expenses incurred while the reservist is on active duty.

Colorado State Tax Credits and Deductions

The state of Colorado also offers tax credits and deductions to military personnel and their families. These include:

  • The Military Service Tax Credit: A tax credit of up to $600 for qualified military pay.
  • The Military Spouse Tax Credit: A tax credit of up to $750 for military spouses.

The Colorado National Guard and Reservists Tax Deductions aim to provide financial relief to those serving in the military. Reservists and their families should research and understand the tax deductions and credits available to them to maximize their benefits.

Key Rules and Regulations

When dealing with IRS guidelines regarding military service, it's crucial to consult the latest version of IRS Publication 3 (Armed Forces' Tax Guide). The general rules stipulate that:

  • Always maintain accurate records of your LES.
  • Understand the difference between taxable and non-taxable income (like BAH and BAS).
  • Know your state of legal residency (SLR) versus your physical duty station.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this applicable to Reservists and National Guard?

Yes, but the rules can vary depending on whether you are activated on Title 10 orders or performing weekend drills. Travel expenses for drills over 100 miles from home may have different deductible statuses.

Does this affect my spouse?

Under the Military Spouse Residency Relief Act (MSRRA), spouses have specific protections regarding where they pay state income tax, which directly intertwines with this topic.
