State Taxes for Active Duty Military in Nebraska

By MilTax Editorial Team • Updated July 12, 2026
Twitter Facebook LinkedIn
[AdSense Placeholder: Top Banner]

As a member of the active duty military, you and your family have already made significant sacrifices in service to your country. When it comes to paying state taxes in Nebraska, you'll be eligible for certain benefits and considerations. Here's what you need to know:

Exemption from State Income Tax

In Nebraska, military personnel are exempt from paying state income tax on their compensation for military service. This exemption applies to both active duty and Reserve military members. However, this exemption only applies to income earned within the state of Nebraska and does not apply to other types of income or investments.

Property Tax Exemption

Active duty military personnel and their families may be eligible for a property tax exemption on their primary residence in Nebraska. This exemption can reduce the amount of property taxes owed on a home, depending on the local tax rates. To qualify, the property must be the primary residence of the military member or their spouse.

Education Benefits

Nebraska offers several education benefits for military personnel and their families. The state participates in the Montgomery GI Bill - Selected Reserve, which provides education benefits for members of the Reserve components. Additionally, the state offers a Troops to Teachers program, which helps military personnel transition to teaching careers in Nebraska.

Other Benefits

  • National Guard and Reserve Tuition Assistance: The state of Nebraska offers tuition assistance for National Guard and Reserve members who are pursuing higher education.
  • Vocational Rehabilitation and Employment: If you're a veteran with a service-connected disability, the state of Nebraska may offer vocational rehabilitation and employment assistance to help you find employment.
  • Housing Assistance: The state of Nebraska offers housing assistance for military personnel and their families, including mortgage assistance and homebuyer programs.

Taxes on Military Retirees

Retired military personnel may be subject to state income tax on their retirement income in Nebraska. However, the state offers a Military Retirement Exemption, which can exempt up to $12,750 per year of retirement income from state income tax.

Special Considerations

If you're a military member or veteran with a disability, you may be eligible for special tax considerations in Nebraska. The state offers a Property Tax Relief Program for eligible veterans, which can help reduce property taxes owed on a primary residence.

Resources

For more information on state taxes and benefits for military personnel and their families in Nebraska, contact the:

  • Nebraska Department of Labor at (402) 465-2200 or www.dol.state.ne.us
  • Nebraska Department of Revenue at (402) 471-2301 or www.revenue.nebraska.gov

Key Rules and Regulations

When dealing with IRS guidelines regarding military service, it's crucial to consult the latest version of IRS Publication 3 (Armed Forces' Tax Guide). The general rules stipulate that:

  • Always maintain accurate records of your LES.
  • Understand the difference between taxable and non-taxable income (like BAH and BAS).
  • Know your state of legal residency (SLR) versus your physical duty station.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this applicable to Reservists and National Guard?

Yes, but the rules can vary depending on whether you are activated on Title 10 orders or performing weekend drills. Travel expenses for drills over 100 miles from home may have different deductible statuses.

Does this affect my spouse?

Under the Military Spouse Residency Relief Act (MSRRA), spouses have specific protections regarding where they pay state income tax, which directly intertwines with this topic.
