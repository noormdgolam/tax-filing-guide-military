South Dakota Military Tax Filing Basics

The state of South Dakota offers a variety of tax benefits and incentives specifically designed for military personnel and their families. Understanding the tax filing basics can help ensure that military personnel and their families take advantage of these benefits and fulfill their tax obligations.

Tax Exemption on Military Pay

  • South Dakota is a state that does not tax military retirement pay or survivor benefits.
  • The state also exempts military pay, including basic allowance for housing (BAH) and basic allowance for subsistence (BAS).

However, military personnel may be subject to state income tax on income earned while not living in South Dakota, such as interest and dividend income.

Tax Credits for Military Personnel

  • The South Dakota Military Tax Credit offers a non-refundable tax credit of up to $3,400 for active-duty military personnel or their spouses who file jointly.
  • To qualify, military personnel must be stationed in South Dakota, and their spouse must be a resident of South Dakota.

The tax credit is applied to the combined tax liability of the military personnel and their spouse, and any remaining credit amount is not refundable.

Tax Filing Obligations for Military Personnel and Their Families

  • As a general rule, military personnel and their families must file a tax return if their total income exceeds the standard deduction for the year.
  • The standard deduction in South Dakota for the 2023 tax year is $5,000 for single filers and $10,000 for joint filers.

However, military personnel and their families may be exempt from filing a tax return if their only income is from military pay and they meet certain other requirements.

Special Tax Filing Instructions for Military Personnel

  • When filing a tax return, military personnel should use Form 1040 and claim the South Dakota Military Tax Credit on Schedule M.
  • Military personnel should also complete Form 8386 and 8332 to claim exemption from state tax on military pay and other benefits.

It is recommended that military personnel and their families consult with a tax professional or use tax preparation software specifically designed for military personnel to ensure compliance with all tax filing requirements and to take advantage of available tax benefits and credits.

Additional Resources

  • The South Dakota Department of Revenue provides information on military tax benefits and credits on its website.
  • The Military OneSource website and the Military and Veterans Affairs section of the IRS website also offer valuable resources for military personnel and their families.

By understanding the tax filing basics in South Dakota, military personnel and their families can ensure compliance with state and federal tax laws and take advantage of available tax benefits and incentives.

Key Rules and Regulations

When dealing with IRS guidelines regarding military service, it's crucial to consult the latest version of IRS Publication 3 (Armed Forces' Tax Guide). The general rules stipulate that:

  • Always maintain accurate records of your LES.
  • Understand the difference between taxable and non-taxable income (like BAH and BAS).
  • Know your state of legal residency (SLR) versus your physical duty station.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this applicable to Reservists and National Guard?

Yes, but the rules can vary depending on whether you are activated on Title 10 orders or performing weekend drills. Travel expenses for drills over 100 miles from home may have different deductible statuses.

Does this affect my spouse?

Under the Military Spouse Residency Relief Act (MSRRA), spouses have specific protections regarding where they pay state income tax, which directly intertwines with this topic.
