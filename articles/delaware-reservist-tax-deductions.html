Delaware National Guard and Reservists Tax Deductions

The Delaware National Guard and Reservists Tax Deductions provide financial relief to military personnel and their families in exchange for their service to the state and country.

Uniformed Services and Homeowners Exemption

The Uniformed Services and Homeowners Exemption is a tax deduction available to Delaware residents who are members of the National Guard or Reserves. In addition to this exemption, residents of Sussex County also qualify for a partial exemption on their real estate tax bill.

To be eligible for the Uniformed Services and Homeowners Exemption, the service member must be an active member of the Delaware National Guard or Reserves and meet the following criteria:

  • Must have completed at least 6 years of active service in the Armed Forces of the United States or the Delaware National Guard or Reserves.
  • Must be currently serving on active duty, be in a reserve component (e.g. National Guard, Army Reserve, Navy Reserve, Air Force Reserve, Marine Corps Reserve, Coast Guard Reserve), or be on inactive duty for training (e.g. Annual Training or AT, or a two-week period each year)
  • Must have an honorable discharge from the Armed Forces of the United States or the Delaware National Guard or Reserves

The application requires documentation, including proof of service, honorable discharge, and proof of residency. The exemption can be applied for through the Delaware State Treasurer's Office.

Delaware Tax Credit for Military Personnel

The Delaware Tax Credit for Military Personnel offers a state tax credit of up to 30% of out-of-state tuition paid by Delaware residents who are active-duty military personnel, National Guard personnel, or Reservists.

To qualify for the credit, the following conditions must be met:

  • The service member must be a resident of Delaware for at least one year immediately preceding the date of filing the tax return.
  • The service member must be an active duty member of the Armed Forces of the United States (including the Delaware National Guard and Reserves)
  • The course of instruction must be at a tax-exempt institution, including state-supported private schools, community colleges, and 4-year institutions

The Delaware Tax Credit for Military Personnel can be applied for on the Delaware Division of Revenue website.

Delaware Homestead Tax Credit

The Delaware Homestead Tax Credit is a tax deduction that provides relief to homeowners who are service members of the National Guard or Reserves. The credit is up to $4,900 annually for residents of Sussex County and up to $1,700 annually for residents in other counties.

The credit requires documentation, including proof of service, and must be applied for by the service member or their spouse. The application can be found on the Delaware State Treasurer's Office website.

Tax laws are subject to change and exemptions are based on current tax credits. Please refer to the Delaware State Treasurer's Office or Division of Revenue website for the most up-to-date information on Delaware National Guard and Reservists Tax Deductions.

Key Rules and Regulations

When dealing with IRS guidelines regarding military service, it's crucial to consult the latest version of IRS Publication 3 (Armed Forces' Tax Guide). The general rules stipulate that:

  • Always maintain accurate records of your LES.
  • Understand the difference between taxable and non-taxable income (like BAH and BAS).
  • Know your state of legal residency (SLR) versus your physical duty station.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this applicable to Reservists and National Guard?

Yes, but the rules can vary depending on whether you are activated on Title 10 orders or performing weekend drills. Travel expenses for drills over 100 miles from home may have different deductible statuses.

Does this affect my spouse?

Under the Military Spouse Residency Relief Act (MSRRA), spouses have specific protections regarding where they pay state income tax, which directly intertwines with this topic.
