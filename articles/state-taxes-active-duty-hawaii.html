Understanding State Taxes for Active Duty Military in Hawaii

Military personnel and their families stationed in Hawaii may be subject to state taxes on their income, but there are certain exemptions and deductions available to them. As a resident of Hawaii, you are subject to the Hawaii state income tax, which is imposed on your gross income.

Exemptions and Tax Credits

  • Active duty military personnel may be exempt from paying state income tax, according to Hawaii Revised Statutes (HRS) 235-10.5. This exemption applies to the military personnel's basic allowance for housing (BAH) and food allowances.
  • However, some forms of income are still taxable, such as salaries, wages, and bonuses.
  • The Hawaii military spouses' state income tax refundable credit is available to eligible spouses. To qualify, the spouse must have been married at the time they were deployed for at least 90 days in the tax year, and their spouse must have been a member of the US military.
  • The state also offers the Disabled Veterans Exemption, which provides a full exemption from state real property taxes for qualified disabled veterans who are 100% disabled or have a service-connected disability.

File Status

Active-duty military personnel can file their tax returns as 'single' or 'head of household' even if they are married. However, if your spouse has a significant source of income, it may be beneficial for them to file jointly.

Form W-2 and Form 1040

Active duty military personnel in Hawaii will receive a Form W-2 from their employer if any state income taxes were withheld. You will report your income and taxes paid on Form 1040. When reporting any income not subject to state taxation, be sure to follow the instructions and note the exemption on the applicable line of your tax return.

Tax Filing Deadline

The tax filing deadline for active-duty military personnel who are Hawaii residents is typically the same as for other Hawaii residents, which is April 17th of each year.

Tax Professionals

When filing taxes, military personnel and their families may consider consulting a tax professional familiar with Hawaii state tax laws. These experts can help you navigate the process and ensure compliance with state and federal tax regulations.

Key Rules and Regulations

When dealing with IRS guidelines regarding military service, it's crucial to consult the latest version of IRS Publication 3 (Armed Forces' Tax Guide). The general rules stipulate that:

  • Always maintain accurate records of your LES.
  • Understand the difference between taxable and non-taxable income (like BAH and BAS).
  • Know your state of legal residency (SLR) versus your physical duty station.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this applicable to Reservists and National Guard?

Yes, but the rules can vary depending on whether you are activated on Title 10 orders or performing weekend drills. Travel expenses for drills over 100 miles from home may have different deductible statuses.

Does this affect my spouse?

Under the Military Spouse Residency Relief Act (MSRRA), spouses have specific protections regarding where they pay state income tax, which directly intertwines with this topic.
