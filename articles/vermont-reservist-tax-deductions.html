Vermont National Guard and Reservists Tax Deductions

The Vermont National Guard and Reservist tax deductions are designed to assist military personnel and their families in reducing their tax liability. These deductions are in addition to the federal tax benefits available to military personnel, such as the federal Reserve Components and National Guard Tax Credit.

Homestead Exemption

The Homestead Exemption is a property tax deduction available to certain National Guard and Reserve members. To qualify, the member must be a homeowner and meet certain income and residency requirements. The exemption can be applied to the state portion of the homestead property taxes and can be claimed on the member's state income tax return.

Personal Income Tax Exemption

The Personal Income Tax Exemption is a state income tax deduction available to National Guard and Reserve members. To qualify, the member must be an active duty, inactive duty for drilling, or a retired National Guard or Reserve member. The exemption is a fixed amount, typically $2,250 or $2,500, and can be claimed on the member's state income tax return.

State Property Tax Credit

The State Property Tax Credit is a property tax deduction available to certain military personnel and their families. To qualify, the member must be a homeowner, meet certain income and residency requirements, and reside in a school district or municipality with a high tax burden. The credit is a fixed amount, typically 25% of the state property taxes paid, and can be claimed on the member's state income tax return.

Special Military Tax Deductions

The Vermont Department of Taxes also offers several special military tax deductions for National Guard and Reserve members and their families. These deductions include:

  • Combat Pay Exemption: Exempts certain combat pay from state income tax.
  • Subsistence Allowance Exemption: Exempts certain subsistence allowances from state income tax.
  • Deployment Pay Exemption: Exempts certain deployment pay from state income tax.

Tax Relief for Military Personnel and Families

Vermont offers various tax relief options for military personnel and their families, including:

  • Tax Filing Relief: Military personnel serving outside Vermont can file their state taxes in Vermont, rather than in the state where they are serving.
  • Exemptions from Certain State Taxes: Military personnel and their families are exempt from certain state taxes, including the state sales tax and the state use tax.
  • Refund Filing Deadlines Extended: Military personnel and their families may have an extended deadline for filing their state income tax returns.

The Vermont Department of Taxes offers a variety of resources for military personnel and their families, including information on tax deductions, credits, and relief options.

To learn more about the tax deductions and relief options available to military personnel and their families in Vermont, visit the Vermont Department of Taxes website or contact a tax professional for assistance.

Key Rules and Regulations

When dealing with IRS guidelines regarding military service, it's crucial to consult the latest version of IRS Publication 3 (Armed Forces' Tax Guide). The general rules stipulate that:

  • Always maintain accurate records of your LES.
  • Understand the difference between taxable and non-taxable income (like BAH and BAS).
  • Know your state of legal residency (SLR) versus your physical duty station.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this applicable to Reservists and National Guard?

Yes, but the rules can vary depending on whether you are activated on Title 10 orders or performing weekend drills. Travel expenses for drills over 100 miles from home may have different deductible statuses.

Does this affect my spouse?

Under the Military Spouse Residency Relief Act (MSRRA), spouses have specific protections regarding where they pay state income tax, which directly intertwines with this topic.
