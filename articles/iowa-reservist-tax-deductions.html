Iowa National Guard and Reservists Tax Deductions

The Iowa National Guard and Reservists are individuals who have made a significant contribution to our country's safety and security. In recognition of their service, the state of Iowa offers various tax deductions to support these brave individuals and their families.

Educational Assistance

  • The National Guard Educational Assistance Program provides educational assistance to Iowa National Guard members. This program offers up to $4,000 per fiscal year for tuition and fees associated with pursuing higher education.
  • The Montgomery G.I. Bill (Chapter 1606) program offers up to 36 months of educational assistance. This includes payment for tuition and fees, as well as a stipend for housing and book expenses.

In addition to these programs, some Iowa institutions of higher learning offer tuition assistance to National Guard members. For example, the University of Iowa offers a reduced tuition rate for Iowa National Guard members. Students are eligible for up to 50% discount in tuition and fee charges at the UI.

Homestead Tax Credit

The Iowa Homestead Tax Credit (HTC) is a non-refundable tax credit that may be taken for the tax year in which an Iowa resident is exempt from paying property taxes. The credit percentage for the 2023 tax year is 30% of the household's net property tax liability.

  • Reservists who are deployed for 90 days or more are eligible for this credit, even if they are no longer residents of Iowa.
  • Servers who received a hardship duty pay may claim the HTC.
  • Individuals who served for more than 24 months overseas, including those deployed for less than 90 days, can claim the credit for two tax years following the year of deployment.

To claim the HTC, you must file an application with the Iowa Department of Revenue and pay the registration fee. You can find additional information and the application on the Iowa Department of Revenue's website.

Property Tax Exemption

The Iowa Property Tax Exemption for Totally and Permanently Disabled Military Veterans and their families, may be eligible to claim a property tax exemption. This exemption applies to property owned by the veteran or their spouse. The exemption amount is up to 100% of the household's net property tax liability.

Other Benefits

  • The State of Iowa provides a reduced rate income tax bracket for certain military and reservist benefits.
  • Iowa also offers a reduced state income tax rate on certain military retirement income earned while serving in the military.
  • Schools in Iowa offer reduced tuition rates to National Guard and veterans. The tuition rate may be 1/3 below the in-state tuition rate.
  • Iowa also offers free hunting and fishing licenses to all active and honorably discharged military personnel, as well as veterans. The license is valid for all fish and game species.

For additional information and tax savings available to Iowa National Guard and Reservists, please visit the Iowa Department of Revenue's website or consult a tax practitioner, certified public accountant, or other tax professional for further guidance.

Key Rules and Regulations

When dealing with IRS guidelines regarding military service, it's crucial to consult the latest version of IRS Publication 3 (Armed Forces' Tax Guide). The general rules stipulate that:

  • Always maintain accurate records of your LES.
  • Understand the difference between taxable and non-taxable income (like BAH and BAS).
  • Know your state of legal residency (SLR) versus your physical duty station.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this applicable to Reservists and National Guard?

Yes, but the rules can vary depending on whether you are activated on Title 10 orders or performing weekend drills. Travel expenses for drills over 100 miles from home may have different deductible statuses.

Does this affect my spouse?

Under the Military Spouse Residency Relief Act (MSRRA), spouses have specific protections regarding where they pay state income tax, which directly intertwines with this topic.
