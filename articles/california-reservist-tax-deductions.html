California National Guard and Reservists Tax Deductions

The California National Guard and Reservists Tax Deductions provide a range of benefits to military personnel and their families. The state of California offers tax deductions and credits to alleviate some of the financial burdens associated with military service.

California National Guard and Reservists Income Tax Credit

  • The California National Guard and Reservists Income Tax Credit offers an annual credit of up to $1,000 to members of the California National Guard and Reservists who are enrolled in the California National Guard's Education and Enlisted Advancement (EEA) programs.
  • To be eligible, applicants must be enrolled in at least 12 units of college credit per term and demonstrate financial need.
  • The credit is designed to help alleviate the financial burden of tuition costs, fees, and other educational expenses for members of the California National Guard and Reservists.
  • The credit is administered by the California Student Aid Commission and can be claimed on the taxpayer's California personal income tax return.

California National Guard and Reservists Property Tax Exemption

California law allows a property tax exemption for the real property of deceased or disabled California National Guard members and Reservists, and their spouses or heirs. The exemption may be claimed for the first $7,000 of the assessed value of the property.

California National Guard and Reservists Mortgage Interest Deduction

Certain military personnel and their spouses may qualify for a mortgage interest deduction on their California state income tax return. To be eligible, taxpayers must meet certain conditions, including:

  • Being on active duty in the military and stationed outside of California
  • Being a surviving spouse, or the spouse of a member of the armed forces who is missing in action or a prisoner of war.
  • Purchasing a residence in California within 12 months of returning to civilian life.

California National Guard and Reservists Education Assistance

The California National Guard and Reservists Education Assistance program offers financial assistance to members of the California National Guard and Reservists, as well as their spouses, dependents, and survivors.

  • Assistance is available for education and training expenses, including tuition, fees, books, and other educational materials.
  • The program is designed to help eligible individuals pursue higher education or vocational training.
  • Assistance may be in the form of grants, scholarships, or other forms of financial aid.

Eligible individuals can apply for education assistance through the California National Guard and Reservists Education Assistance program by contacting their local California National Guard unit or by visiting the program's website.

Other Benefits

In addition to these tax deductions and credits, the state of California offers several other benefits to military personnel and their families, including access to low-interest home loans, priority registration for California State University (CSU) and University of California (UC) courses, and special licenses and permits for active duty military and their dependents.

For more information on the California National Guard and Reservists Tax Deductions and other benefits for military personnel and their families, contact the California State Controller's Office or visit the program's website.

Key Rules and Regulations

When dealing with IRS guidelines regarding military service, it's crucial to consult the latest version of IRS Publication 3 (Armed Forces' Tax Guide). The general rules stipulate that:

  • Always maintain accurate records of your LES.
  • Understand the difference between taxable and non-taxable income (like BAH and BAS).
  • Know your state of legal residency (SLR) versus your physical duty station.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this applicable to Reservists and National Guard?

Yes, but the rules can vary depending on whether you are activated on Title 10 orders or performing weekend drills. Travel expenses for drills over 100 miles from home may have different deductible statuses.

Does this affect my spouse?

Under the Military Spouse Residency Relief Act (MSRRA), spouses have specific protections regarding where they pay state income tax, which directly intertwines with this topic.
