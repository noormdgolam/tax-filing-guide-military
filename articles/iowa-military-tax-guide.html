Iowa Military Tax Filing Basics

The Iowa state government provides special tax benefits for military service members and their families. These tax benefits aim to ease the financial burden that comes with serving in the military. Understanding these benefits can help you make informed decisions about your taxes and take advantage of the opportunities available to you.

Homestead Tax Credit and Military Exemption for Active Duty Military

The Iowa Code provides a military exemption for active duty military personnel. Iowa's Military Code, Chapter 35B, provides an exemption for the assessed value of property owned by an individual or spouse who is on active duty military service and living outside of the state. Additionally, Iowa's Homestead Tax Credit can be applied to property owned by active duty military personnel who reside in the state.

  • Must be on active duty military service
  • Home must be owned and occupied by the individual or spouse
  • The property must be subject to Iowa property tax
  • The exemption only applies to the property where the individual or spouse resides, not other properties they may own within the state

Special Exemptions for Disabled Veterans

Iowa also provides special exemptions for disabled veterans. If you have a service-related disability rating, you may qualify for additional exemptions on your Iowa property taxes. This is known as the "Disabled Veterans Property Tax Reduction."

  • Must have a 100% disability rating from the VA
  • Must be the owner of record of the property in question
  • Home must be the veteran's primary residence

State Tax Benefits for Military Retirement Income (MRT-4)

Iowa provides a special exemption for a portion of military retirement income received by Iowa residents. This is known as the "Military Retirement Tax Exemption" (MRT-4)

The exemption applies to military retirement income received from any branch of the U.S. Armed Forces, including the Reserves and National Guard.

  • $2,000 exemption for single filers
  • $4,000 exemption for joint filing status

The tax exemption only applies to Iowa state taxes and does not apply to federal taxes.

Other Benefits

Iowa also provides a few other tax-related benefits for its military personnel, including:

  • Soldiers' and Sailors' Civil Relief Act: Iowa state law provides relief from property taxes and other state and local obligations for military personnel called to active duty.
  • State income tax credits for certain military benefits: The state income tax credit for disabled veterans, surviving spouses of deceased service members, and families who have had a family member killed in action or who have had a family member die while on active duty may also be claimed on the Iowa individual income tax form.

It's essential to consult with a tax professional to ensure you're taking advantage of all the tax benefits available to you as a military service member or veteran in Iowa. They can provide you with more information on these benefits and assist you with the tax preparation process.

Key Rules and Regulations

When dealing with IRS guidelines regarding military service, it's crucial to consult the latest version of IRS Publication 3 (Armed Forces' Tax Guide). The general rules stipulate that:

  • Always maintain accurate records of your LES.
  • Understand the difference between taxable and non-taxable income (like BAH and BAS).
  • Know your state of legal residency (SLR) versus your physical duty station.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this applicable to Reservists and National Guard?

Yes, but the rules can vary depending on whether you are activated on Title 10 orders or performing weekend drills. Travel expenses for drills over 100 miles from home may have different deductible statuses.

Does this affect my spouse?

Under the Military Spouse Residency Relief Act (MSRRA), spouses have specific protections regarding where they pay state income tax, which directly intertwines with this topic.
