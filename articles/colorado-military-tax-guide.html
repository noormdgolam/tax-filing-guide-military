Colorado Military Tax Filing Basics

As a military personnel or a family member, navigating tax season can be challenging, especially with unique situations involving deployment, multiple residences, and changing income. Understanding Colorado's military tax filing basics is essential to ensure you take advantage of available credits, deductions, and exemptions.

COLORADO'S MILITARY TAX BREAKS -

  • State Exemption: Military personnel and spouses may qualify for Colorado state income tax exemptions in specific situations.
  • Tax Credits for Military Retirees: Retired military personnel can claim a state tax credit of up to $250 for every $5,000 of Colorado taxable income over $20,000.
  • Soldier's and Veteran's Disability Benefits Tax Exemption: Disability benefits from the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs are not subject to Colorado state income tax.

TIPS FOR MILITARY-TAXPAYERS IN COLORADO-

  • Choose the Right Filing Status: Service members, spouses, and dependents may claim Head of Household or Single filing status.
  • Report Deployed Income: Report income earned during deployment, but keep in mind it may be exempt from federal and state taxes.
  • Homes in Multiple Places: Claim residency in Colorado only if you spend more time here than in another location.

WHAT IF I'M DEPLOYED DURING TAX FILING?

You're not alone. Colorado provides resources and deadlines for military personnel with deployed income. Be aware that if you were deployed for more than half of the tax year, your income may be tax-exempt at the federal and state levels.

GET HELP WITH YOUR COLORADO TAX FILING-

  • Colorado Department of Taxation and Finance website
  • Reach out to a local tax preparer who has experience working with military personnel and families.
  • Contact the IRS Military and Veterans Organizations

Remember, tax laws and regulations may have changed since your last filing. Consult a tax professional or check with the state department of taxation and finance to ensure you take advantage of all eligible tax credits, deductions, and exemptions available to you and your family.

Key Rules and Regulations

When dealing with IRS guidelines regarding military service, it's crucial to consult the latest version of IRS Publication 3 (Armed Forces' Tax Guide). The general rules stipulate that:

  • Always maintain accurate records of your LES.
  • Understand the difference between taxable and non-taxable income (like BAH and BAS).
  • Know your state of legal residency (SLR) versus your physical duty station.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this applicable to Reservists and National Guard?

Yes, but the rules can vary depending on whether you are activated on Title 10 orders or performing weekend drills. Travel expenses for drills over 100 miles from home may have different deductible statuses.

Does this affect my spouse?

Under the Military Spouse Residency Relief Act (MSRRA), spouses have specific protections regarding where they pay state income tax, which directly intertwines with this topic.
