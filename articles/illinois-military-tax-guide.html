Illinois Military Tax Filing Basics

Military Tax Benefits in Illinois

Military personnel and their families stationed in Illinois may be eligible for various tax benefits. The Illinois Department of Revenue and the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) offer tax credits, deductions, and exemptions that can help reduce income tax liabilities.

Illinois Military Exemption

  • An Illinois resident on active duty with a military base in Illinois is exempt from paying state income tax on their military pay.
  • Military personnel are also exempt from state income tax on combat pay, special duty pay, and hazardous duty pay. However, combat pay received for combat in foreign service is subject to state income tax.
  • Certain members of the Illinois National Guard may be exempt from state income tax on pay received for service.

Illinois Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)

The Illinois EITC is a refundable credit that provides tax relief to low- to moderate-income individuals, including military personnel and their families.

  • Eligibility for the Illinois EITC depends on factors such as income, filing status, and the number of dependents.
  • Military personnel who file a joint return and have at least one qualifying child may be eligible for the maximum EITC amount of $1,050.
  • Those who file a joint return but do not have qualifying children may be eligible for a smaller amount of up to $200.

Soldiers' and Sailors' Civil Relief Act (SSCRA)

  • The SSCRA allows eligible military personnel to defer their Illinois income tax payments, sales tax payments, and certain fines during military service.
  • Eligibility for relief under the SSCRA depends on military service, income limits, and financial hardship.

Illinois Tax Credits for Military Personnel

The Illinois Department of Veterans Affairs offers tax credits for military personnel who purchase a primary residence in Illinois.

  • The Home Buyer Tax Credit provides a one-time credit of up to $7,500 for military personnel buying a home in Illinois.
  • The Homebuyer Down Payment Assistance Program offers up to $15,000 in down payment assistance for military personnel purchasing a home in Illinois.

How to File a Tax Return in Illinois

Military personnel and their families can file a tax return in Illinois using the same forms as civilian taxpayers. It's recommended to consult with a tax professional or the Illinois Department of Revenue website for more information on tax filing requirements and deadlines.

The IRS provides free tax filing assistance through its Volunteer Income Tax Assistance (VITA) and Tax Counseling for the Elderly (TCE) programs. Eligible military personnel and their families may qualify for these services, which can help with tax preparation and filing.

The Illinois Department of Revenue website offers tax resources and information for military personnel and their families. Military personnel and their families can also contact the IRS at 800-829-1040 for more information on tax credits and deductions.

Key Rules and Regulations

When dealing with IRS guidelines regarding military service, it's crucial to consult the latest version of IRS Publication 3 (Armed Forces' Tax Guide). The general rules stipulate that:

  • Always maintain accurate records of your LES.
  • Understand the difference between taxable and non-taxable income (like BAH and BAS).
  • Know your state of legal residency (SLR) versus your physical duty station.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this applicable to Reservists and National Guard?

Yes, but the rules can vary depending on whether you are activated on Title 10 orders or performing weekend drills. Travel expenses for drills over 100 miles from home may have different deductible statuses.

Does this affect my spouse?

Under the Military Spouse Residency Relief Act (MSRRA), spouses have specific protections regarding where they pay state income tax, which directly intertwines with this topic.
