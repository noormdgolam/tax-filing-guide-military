Maryland Military Tax Filing Basics

Military personnel and their families stationed in Maryland may be eligible for certain tax benefits, exemptions, and deductions. Understanding these tax filing basics can help you navigate the tax season with confidence.

Taxes for Military Personnel in Maryland

The Maryland state tax authority, Comptroller of Maryland, treats income from military service as part of your Maryland taxable income. This includes your basic pay, allowances, and other forms of income.

MD Military Exemptions and Deductions

As a Maryland military member or family, you may qualify for the following exemptions and deductions:

  • Military Personal Property Tax Relief - Up to $30,000 of the assessed value of your primary residence or a vehicle is exempt from state tax.
  • Uniform Service Member's Civil Relief Act - A temporary reduction in interest rates on mortgages and credit card debt may be available to active duty service members.
  • Special Pay Deduction - Maryland allows a deduction for Special Pays, such as flight pay, hazardous pay, and hostile fire pay, from your state taxable income.
  • Reimbursement for Relocation Expenses - The federal government offers tax-free reimbursements for relocation expenses, which include the moving of household goods, vehicles, and other personal property.
  • Disability Pay for Maryland Residents - Maryland is one of four states that exempt the first $15,000 of non-Service-Connected Disability Retirement Pay (SCDRP) from state tax.

Additionally, Maryland military families can benefit from tax-free reimbursement for moving expenses and certain types of military pay that are nontaxable by the federal government.

Reporting Military Income on Maryland Tax Forms

When filing your Maryland tax return, you will report your military income on the same form used for your federal tax return, Form 1099-MISC. Maryland Form M-1099 is used to report miscellaneous income, including non-salary income, such as combat pay or housing allowances.

Form W-2 and Federal Tax Forms

You'll also need a copy of your federal Form W-2 to report your Maryland income. If you receive Form 1099-R or W-2G, these forms will be used to report any military pay and allowances.

Military Tax Assistance

The Maryland Comptroller's Office offers free tax assistance to Maryland residents who are active or retired military personnel, including a free state tax clinic for those with low- to moderate-income.

Other Maryland Military Tax Benefits

Maryland offers other tax benefits for military personnel and families, such as:

  • Veteran's Tax Credit - Maryland offers a tax credit of up to $5,000 for qualified veterans, including those with combat-related disabilities or those who served during wartime.
  • Military and Veteran Property Tax Relief - Maryland law provides property tax relief to active-duty personnel serving in combat zones and their property owners.

Take advantage of these Maryland military tax benefits, exemptions, and deductions to ensure you're taking advantage of all the tax relief you're eligible for. Consult your tax professional or refer to Maryland state tax laws and regulations for more information.

Key Rules and Regulations

When dealing with IRS guidelines regarding military service, it's crucial to consult the latest version of IRS Publication 3 (Armed Forces' Tax Guide). The general rules stipulate that:

  • Always maintain accurate records of your LES.
  • Understand the difference between taxable and non-taxable income (like BAH and BAS).
  • Know your state of legal residency (SLR) versus your physical duty station.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this applicable to Reservists and National Guard?

Yes, but the rules can vary depending on whether you are activated on Title 10 orders or performing weekend drills. Travel expenses for drills over 100 miles from home may have different deductible statuses.

Does this affect my spouse?

Under the Military Spouse Residency Relief Act (MSRRA), spouses have specific protections regarding where they pay state income tax, which directly intertwines with this topic.
