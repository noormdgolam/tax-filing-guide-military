Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profiles
Specific Considerations for Rank Profile 97

This tax filing guide is specifically designed for military personnel with Rank Profile 97. The following information outlines the key tax considerations, exemptions, and deductions available to Military personnel with this rank profile.

Exemptions:
  1. Exemption from State and Local Income Tax Military personnel stationed in states where they do not reside may not be subject to state and local income tax.
  2. Exemption from Property Tax Military personnel may be exempt from property tax on their primary residence while stationed in a new location.
Deductions:
  1. Special Duty Allowance Military personnel with a special duty allowance are eligible for deductions based on the amount of the allowance.
  2. Mortgage Interest Deduction Military personnel may claim a mortgage interest deduction on their primary residence.

It is essential to note that tax laws and regulations can change. Military personnel should consult with tax professionals or the IRS to ensure they are aware of the latest tax laws and regulations.

To claim exemptions and deductions, military personnel with Rank Profile 97 must complete and submit the following forms:

  • Form 1040: U.S. Individual Income Tax Return
  • Form 8820: Orphan Benefits (if applicable)
  • Form 8822: Change of Address (if applicable)
Additional Resources:

For more information on tax filing and tax law, military personnel can visit the following resources:

  • IRS Military Tax Center
  • Defense Finance and Accounting Service (DFAS)
  • Defense Tax Information
