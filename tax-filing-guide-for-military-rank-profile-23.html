Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profile 23

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

The tax filing process can be complex and overwhelming for military personnel, especially those with unique circumstances, such as those serving in a Combat Zone (CZ) or Combat Zone Bonus (CZB) programs. As a Military Rank 23, it's essential to understand the specific tax filing requirements and rules applicable to your situation.

Tax Filing Deadlines

The standard tax filing deadline for military personnel is typically April 15th of each year. However, if you're serving in a Combat Zone, your deadline may be extended to the 180th day after leaving the zone. If you're a qualifying resident of a US territory, such as Puerto Rico, the deadline is June 15th. Additionally, if you're experiencing a hardship or have a valid reason, you may be eligible for an automatic six-month extension, which would extend your deadline to October 15th.

Tax Filing Status

As a Military Rank 23, you're considered a single filer for tax purposes, unless you're married and file jointly with your spouse. If you're married but filing separately, you and your spouse must both agree to file separately. Filing jointly typically offers more tax benefits than filing separately, but it also means that you and your spouse are jointly and severally liable for any taxes owed.

Tax Deductions and Credits

Active-duty military personnel, including Military Rank 23, are eligible for several tax deductions and credits that can help reduce your tax liability. Some of the most significant tax benefits include:

  • The Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH) is tax-free and can be used to reduce your total tax liability.
  • The Military Spouse Residency Relief Act (MSRRA) allows you to claim your spouse as a dependent on your tax return, even if they live in a different state.
  • The Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) can provide a refundable tax credit to low- to moderate-income working individuals and families, including those in the military.
  • The Moving Expense Deduction allows you to deduct up to $25,000 of unreimbursed moving expenses if you're an active-duty military member on a permanent change of station (PCS).

Tax Returns and Forms

The primary tax forms used by Military Rank 23 are the Form 1040 and the Schedule A. You'll need to complete Form 1040 to report your income and calculate your tax liability, and Schedule A to claim your deductions.

Additionally, you may need to file the following forms:

  • Form W-2 to report your military pay and other income.
  • Form 1099 to report interest, dividends, and capital gains.
  • Form 1040-5 to claim your military housing allowance (if not already included in your Form 1040).

It's essential to consult with a tax professional or use tax preparation software to ensure you're using the correct forms and taking advantage of all the tax benefits available to you as a Military Rank 23.
