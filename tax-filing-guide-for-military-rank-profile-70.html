Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profile 70

The tax filing process for members of the Military serving at Rank Profile 70 presents a unique set of circumstances, influenced by military pay and specific deductions. As an enlisted member of the US Armed Forces at the rank of E-6 (Staff Sergeant) or above, and meeting the specific criteria outlined in the Military Retirement Pension plan, individuals fall under a distinct tax profile.

Tax Filing Requirements

Members at Rank Profile 70 typically have several factors influencing their tax obligations. These include:

  • Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH): As a result of being housed in a Military barracks or other Military-owned facility, a specific amount of housing expenses is reimbursed to the servicemember through their BAH. This reduces their taxable income, as they're not paying for housing and the reimbursement amount isn't taxed.
  • Allowances and Incentives: Taxpayers may receive several allowances and incentives, such as the Basic Allowance for Subsistence (BAS) for meals, as well as other special pay and allowances. These are nontaxable, and don't contribute to taxable income.
  • Foreign Earned Income Exclusion: Military personnel serving abroad may be exempt from taxes on income they earn from a foreign source, if they meet specific residency and other requirements, which can greatly impact their federal tax burden.
  • Pension and Savings Contributions: Military members who contribute to the Thrift Savings Plan (similar to a 401(k) for civilians) and those who have earned a Military retirement pension, can claim tax deductions or credits.

Key Considerations for Filing Taxes

Servicemembers must also be aware of their Military-specific tax rules:

  • Military Deduction: Members are allowed a standard deduction of up to $350, with varying amounts depending on family status and other factors.
  • Child Tax Credit: Servicemembers are eligible for the Child Tax Credit, with an increased benefit of up to $3,000 per child, as part of the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act.
  • Prior Year Adjustments: Members with prior-year issues may need to make adjustments for prior-year tax returns, especially if they had to use a "Where You Are" filing status.

Additional Filing Tips

When preparing tax returns for Military Rank Profile 70 members:

  • Contact the Military Personnel Finance Center for questions or concerns related to military pay and allowances.
  • Use the free tax software from the Military OneSource, which may assist in identifying Military-specific tax benefits.
  • Keep meticulous records of Military income, allowances, and incentives, as well as other tax-related documents.

Tax Filing Deadlines and Resources

Members with Military tax queries or concerns can contact:

  • The Military Tax Guide hotline (1-800-342-1057).
  • The IRS Taxpayer Assistance Centers for in-person visits.
  • The IRS website (irs.gov) for online tax resources and e-file options.

Penalties for Late Tax Filing

Members failing to meet the tax deadline may face penalties and additional interest on any owed taxes. It is essential to file before the deadline or apply for an automatic six-month extension if applicable.
