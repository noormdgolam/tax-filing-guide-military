Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profile 103

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

As a member of the military with a rank profile 103, you may be entitled to certain tax benefits and deductions. Tax filing can be complex, but understanding your specific situation and the rules that apply to you can help you maximize your refunds and comply with tax laws.

Tax Forms and Filing Information

The U.S. military, including those with a rank profile 103, use the Form 1040 series to report their income. You may need to complete additional forms, such as Form 4852, Statement of Changes in File Status and Income, or Form 2106, Employee Business Expenses.

As a military member, you're entitled to certain deductions and credits. You may be able to claim the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC), and the Combat Zone tax deduction. The military's combat zone and hazardous duty pay are also tax-free. You can find more information about these and other military tax benefits on the IRS website.

It's essential to keep accurate records of your income and expenses throughout the year, including military allowances like Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH) and Basic Allowance for Subsistence (BAS). These allowances don't have to be reported as income on your tax return.

Self-Employment and Business Expenses

If you earn income from a military side business, you're considered self-employed and must report this income on Schedule C (Form 1040). You may also be able to deduct business expenses related to your military side business, such as a home office deduction or business travel expenses.

Keep accurate records of your business income and expenses throughout the year. Use a separate bank account to record your business transactions, and maintain a log of your business miles driven and any other expenses.

Dependent Information

As a military member, you're allowed to claim a dependent for tax purposes even if you're not married. You may be able to claim a dependent if you're the sole support of your dependents, or if they rely on you for more than half of their support.

Include the Social Security number or Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN) of your dependent on your tax return, as well as their relationship to you and their age.

Tax Filing Deadlines

The standard tax filing deadline is April 15 every year. However, military members stationed abroad may have an automatic six-month filing extension to October 15.

File your tax return on time to avoid penalties and interest on any unpaid taxes. Use Form 4868 to request an automatic six-month extension if you need more time to file. You can e-file or request a paper copy of this form through the IRS website.

Professional Assistance

For accurate and reliable tax guidance, consider hiring a tax professional who's experienced in preparing tax returns for military members. They can help ensure you receive all the deductions and credits you're eligible for and avoid potential errors.

Be sure to select a tax professional experienced in military taxation and familiar with tax laws that apply to military members.
