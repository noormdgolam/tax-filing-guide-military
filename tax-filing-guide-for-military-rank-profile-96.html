Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profile 96

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

A tax filing guide specifically designed for Military Rank Profile 96:

Tax Credits and Deductions for Military Members

As a member of the armed forces with a Military Rank Profile 96, you may be eligible for several tax credits and deductions that can help reduce your tax liability. These include:

  • Military Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH) - This tax-exempt allowance is used to offset the cost of housing when stationed abroad or at a remote or temporary duty station.
  • Imminent Danger Pay (IDP) and Hostile Fire Pay (HFP) - These tax-exempt allowances are provided to members serving in designated combat zones or areas deemed hostile or dangerous.
  • Military Combat Zone Tax Exclusion - This provision allows members serving in a combat zone to exclude a portion of their military pay from taxation.
  • Tax-Free Moving Expenses - Members who receive orders to move due to military service may be eligible for tax-free moving expenses.

In addition to these credits and deductions, Military Rank Profile 96 members may also be eligible for other tax benefits, including:

Eligibility for the Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)Eligibility

As a military member, you may be eligible for the EITC, which provides a refundable tax credit to low- to moderate-income workers.

Exemptions from Self-Employment TaxExemptions

As a member of the armed forces, you are exempt from self-employment tax on military pay and allowances.

Tax Filing Obligations and Forms

As a Military Rank Profile 96 member, you must file a tax return with the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) annually, reporting your income and claiming any credits and deductions to which you are eligible. The following tax forms and documents are required:

  • Federal Tax Return Form 1040 - File this form annually to report your income and claim any tax credits and deductions. You may also need to file Form 1040A or Form 1040EZ, depending on your income level.
  • Military Tax Documents - You will receive a Form W-2 from your military pay office, which shows your military pay and allowances. You may also need to obtain a Form 1098 from your lender to claim mortgage interest deductions.
  • Proof of Military Service - You may need to provide proof of military service, such as a DD-214, to claim certain tax credits and deductions.

It is essential to keep accurate records of your military pay and allowances, as well as any tax documents and forms received from your military pay office. Consult with a tax professional or the IRS to ensure you are taking advantage of all the tax credits and deductions available to you.
