Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profile 52

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Rank Profile 52 Tax Filing Guide
Tax Filing Requirements Key Considerations
The Internal Revenue Code (IRC) requires military personnel to file tax returns in the same manner as civilian taxpayers. The standard tax filing deadline remains April 15th unless an extension is filed. Rank Profile 52 personnel may be eligible for special tax benefits, including the Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (FEIE) and the Foreign Tax Credit. Additionally, they may be eligible for the Combat Zone Exclusion, which can reduce taxable income earned in designated combat zones.
Tax Filing Status Single, Married Filing Jointly, Married Filing Separately, Head of Household, or Qualifying Widow(er)
Depreciation of Military Equipment and Vehicles Rank Profile 52 personnel can depreciate the cost of military equipment and vehicles, such as aircraft and tanks, on their tax returns. This can result in a reduction in taxable income and lower tax liability.
Foreign Earnings and Tax Credits A rank Profile 52 taxpayer who lives and works abroad may be eligible for the FEIE, which can exclude up to $105,900 of their foreign-earned income from taxation. Additionally, they may be eligible for the Foreign Tax Credit, which can offset taxes paid to foreign governments.
Combat Zone Exclusion The Combat Zone Exclusion reduces taxable income earned by military personnel in designated combat zones. For Tax Year 2022, the exclusion is capped at $3,300.

Form 8853, Part-Year Interest-Free and/or Low-Interest Credit

A military member whose living expenses in a combat zone are paid for by the U.S. government or a military allowance, but who has interest-free or low-interest credit available to them, may be able to claim a credit. The credit is allowed for one or more years of tax liability on that amount.
  1. Create Form 8853, Part-Year Interest-Free and/or Low-Interest Credit and include the following:
  2. Line 1: Enter the gross amount of your qualified residence interest (QRI) for the taxable year.
  3. Line 2: If you are a rank Profile 52 taxpayer who has claimed the QRI deduction in the current year, subtract the QRI deduction claimed on your return from the QRI claimed on this form, and enter the result on line 3.
  4. Line 4: Enter the gross amount of qualified residence interest (QRI) claimed on Schedule A or Form 8814 for the current year.
  5. Line 5: Calculate the tax savings by subtracting the QRI claimed on line 4 from your gross income for the current year.
  6. Complete the rest of the form according to the instructions.

Form 4562, Depreciation and Amortization (Including Information on Listed Property)

This is typically included for tax preparation purposes to account for depreciation of military property like aircraft and tanks.
