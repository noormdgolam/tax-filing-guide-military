Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profile 98

Military personnel serving in the United States Armed Forces often have unique tax considerations due to their military income and relocation. In this tax filing guide, we'll focus on the specific filing requirements and considerations for military rank profile 98.

  • E-8 to E-9 Enlisted and Equivalent Officer Ranks**: This tax filing guide is designed for E-8 to E-9 enlisted personnel and equivalent officer ranks.
  • For the 2023 tax year, military personnel filing as joint returns with their spouse must meet the income requirements and have a valid Social Security number for each filer.
  • Individuals with multiple sources of income, including active duty, civilian, and retirement pay, need to understand how to report and allocate each component of their income accurately.

Tax Filing Status

Military personnel may file their taxes using one of the following statuses:

  • Single**: Individuals who are unmarried and do not claim a dependent may file as single.
  • Joint**: Filing jointly with a spouse is usually more advantageous than filing separately, but certain situations may necessitate separate filing.
  • Head of Household**: Military personnel who qualify as head of household may claim a lower tax rate than single filers.

Taxable Military Income

The majority of military income is nontaxable, but there are some exceptions. Taxable military income includes:

  • Basic Pay**: Only the amount exceeding $3,950 in 2023 is subject to federal income tax.
  • Overt pay (Basic Allowance for Overt pay for hazardous duties)
  • Combat pay (special tax allowance for hazardous duty pay)

Form Reporting Requirements

Military personnel must report their income using the following forms:

  • W-2: For reporting civilian and military gross income as well as payroll withholdings.
  • 1095-B: Health insurance coverage statement.
  • 1098-T: Educational assistance statement, if applicable.

Military personnel should maintain complete records and documentation to substantiate their tax filings. If they need further clarification on any of these points or to seek tax advice from the Military and Veterans Tax Center, they can access tax.gov/military or contact their tax professional.
