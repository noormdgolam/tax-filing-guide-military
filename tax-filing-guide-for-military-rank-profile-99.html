Tax Filing Guide for Military Rank Profile 99

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Tax law and regulations can be complex, especially for those in the military. Members of the military have unique circumstances that affect their tax obligations, including tax filing deadlines, residency status, and special deductions and credits.

In this section, we will guide Military Rank Profile 99 personnel through tax filing requirements, deductions, and credits that can reduce their taxable income.

Tax Filing Deadlines for Military Personnel

For Military Rank Profile 99 personnel who are serving outside the United States or its territories during tax season, an automatic extension to file their tax return is available until June 15th following the tax year.

Additionally, the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) recommends filing as soon as possible after returning to the United States to avoid processing delays and to receive any tax refunds sooner.

Residency Status for Military Personnel

Military Rank Profile 99 personnel are generally considered residents of the state in which they are permanently stationed for tax purposes.

However, they may be considered non-residents in the state they are currently stationed in if they meet specific requirements, such as having a temporary duty (TDY) assignment or being deployed overseas.

Deductions and Credits for Military Personnel

Military Rank Profile 99 personnel may be eligible for various tax deductions and credits to reduce their taxable income.

  • Military Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH): The IRS allows military personnel to exclude certain housing costs, including BAH, from their gross income.

  • Basic Allowance for Subsistence (BAS): Military personnel can deduct BAS, which covers their food expenses while on duty, from their gross income.

  • Military Uniform Allowance: Military personnel can deduct expenses related to wearing a uniform on duty from their gross income.

  • Movements Reimbursement: If Military Rank Profile 99 personnel incur transportation expenses associated with PCS moves, they can claim moving expenses on their tax return.

Additional Resources for Military Personnel

The IRS provides a variety of resources for military personnel to help with tax-filing requirements, including:

  • Tax Help for Military Homeowners: Provides tax guidance for military personnel who own or rent homes.

  • Military OneSource: A service that provides tax information, financial assistance, and other services to military personnel and their families.

  • Military Financial Aid: Offers tax-related financial assistance to military personnel.

Conclusion

Military Rank Profile 99 personnel have unique circumstances that affect their tax obligations, including tax filing deadlines, residency status, and special deductions and credits.

By understanding their tax-filing requirements and utilizing available resources, Military Rank Profile 99 personnel can ensure they are taking advantage of all available tax savings and avoiding potential penalties.

It is highly recommended that Military Rank Profile 99 personnel consult a tax professional or seek guidance from Military OneSource to ensure accuracy and completeness of their tax filing.
